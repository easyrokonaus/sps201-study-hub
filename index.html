Quick Revision Cheat Sheet

AOD exam focus: harm minimisation · stigma · screening tools · treatment · policy (McClelland).

Harm minimisation

Australia uses three pillars: demand reduction, supply reduction, and harm reduction. Goal is to reduce harm even if use continues. Examples: brief intervention, needle programs, naloxone, safer settings.

Screening tools

  • AUDIT — alcohol; 10 items; risk levels.
  • ASSIST — several substances; guides next steps.
  • CAGE / CAGE-AID — 4 questions (cut down, annoyed, guilty, eye-opener); AID adds drugs.

Screening finds risk, not a full diagnosis.

Stigma

Public stigma — negative attitudes from others. Self-stigma — internalised shame. Stigma can reduce help-seeking. Use person-first language and trauma-informed care.

Treatment & rehab

Detox / withdrawal management — medical support to stop safely. Rehab — longer programs with skills and relapse prevention. Pharmacotherapy — e.g. opioid treatment medications. Detox alone is rarely enough; plan aftercare.

Policy (McClelland)

Influence depends on access, resources, and relationships. Business often has strong access. Peak bodies represent many organisations. Welfare groups may balance advocacy with funding. Coalitions and evidence strengthen advocacy.
